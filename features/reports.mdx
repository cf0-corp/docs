Reports lets you convert a Lab research thread into a publication-ready PDF. Once you have completed a research conversation in Lab, cf0 uses that thread's analysis, charts, and citations to generate a structured equity research report. You can edit the draft inline before exporting a branded PDF to share with your team or clients.

## What a report includes

Each generated report contains:

- A cover page with your firm's branding
- An investment summary and key metrics
- Charts and data tables drawn from your Lab conversation
- Inline citations linked to the original source documents
- Standard report sections (thesis, financials, risks, disclosure)

## Generate a report from a Lab thread

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run your research in Lab">
    Complete a research conversation in [Lab](/features/lab). The more detailed the thread, the richer the generated report will be.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Reports panel">
    In Lab, click **Generate report** in the thread toolbar. cf0 reads the conversation and drafts the report structure automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and edit the draft">
    The draft opens in an inline editor. Click any section to edit the text directly. Charts and tables pulled from your Lab thread appear in place and are not editable — they reflect the live data from your analysis.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export as PDF">
    Click **Export PDF**. cf0 renders the report with your organisation's branding — logo, colours, and font — and downloads the file to your browser.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The more targeted your Lab thread, the tighter the generated report. Use a dedicated thread per company or thesis rather than mixing multiple topics in one conversation.
</Tip>

## The Reports dashboard

The **Reports** page (accessible from the left sidebar) lists every report you have generated.

From the dashboard you can:

- **View** any report by clicking its row — the report opens in a side panel.
- **Go to thread** to jump back to the original Lab conversation that generated the report.
- **Delete** a report to remove it permanently.

<Note>
  Deleting a report does not delete the original Lab thread. The thread remains accessible in Lab.
</Note>

## Branding

Reports inherit your organisation's branding automatically. Org admins configure the firm name, logo, and colour scheme in **Settings**. Every exported PDF reflects those settings without any manual formatting work.
