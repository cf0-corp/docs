cf0 is **the financial intelligence stack**. Three layers, one moat.

**Data** is what your firm reads: SEC filings, earnings transcripts, customer comms, internal docs. **Brain** compounds those sources into a wiki that knows your customers, deals, and decisions — and remembers across sessions. **Lab** is where you ask it anything, with agents pulling from the brain and on-demand UI rendering the answer in seconds.

Each layer is a defensible product on its own. The compounding effect — Data feeding Brain feeding Lab that ships cited reports — is the part competitors can't replicate by gluing tools together.

## How a research session runs

1. **Ask in Lab.** Type a question in plain English — for example, *"Compare NVDA and AMD gross margin trajectory over the last eight quarters."* Lab routes the query through the brain, pulls the relevant filings and transcripts, and streams structured output: chart, table, narrative.
2. **Verify every number.** Each figure links back to its source — the exact filing, page, and section. Click to inspect provenance directly.
3. **Generate a report.** When the thread is tight enough to share, ask Lab to generate a report. cf0 assembles a branded PDF with sections, charts, and inline citations.
4. **Encode it as a Skill.** If the workflow is one you'll run again, save it as a Skill. Your whole team can run it with one slash-command.

<Note>
cf0 never trains on your data and never shares it across organisations. Filings, documents, and reports are scoped to your org workspace. Access controls run at the org level via Clerk.
</Note>
