---
title: "Track research activity in your CF0 dashboard"
description: "The CF0 Dashboard shows your personal research activity, usage metrics, and weekly streak. Org admins get a second tab with team-level usage."
---

The Dashboard gives you a live view of your research activity on CF0. Open it from the left navigation bar. You'll see usage metrics, a weekly activity streak, and a summary of your recent queries — all updated automatically as you work.

## Personal dashboard

Every CF0 user has a personal dashboard showing their own activity:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Usage metrics" icon="bar-chart-2">
    A breakdown of your queries, reports generated, and data sources accessed over recent periods.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Activity streak" icon="flame">
    A week-by-week streak tracker showing the days you have been active on CF0. Build a streak by using Lab, running reports, or browsing filings on consecutive days.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Recent queries" icon="clock">
    A log of your recent Lab queries and reports so you can quickly return to past research threads.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Research summary" icon="layers">
    An at-a-glance view of the companies and topics you have researched most recently.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Org dashboard (admins only)

If you are an org admin, you see a second tab — **Org dashboard** — alongside your personal view. The org dashboard aggregates activity across all members of your organisation:

- Total queries and reports generated by the team.
- Per-member activity summaries.
- Usage trends over time.

<Note>
  Only org admins can access the org dashboard. Members see only their own personal dashboard.
</Note>

## Usage and billing

The dashboard is for visibility only. CF0 does not bill based on query volume or usage. Billing is seat-based — one flat rate per active analyst per month, regardless of how much or how little each seat is used. High-volume researchers and occasional users cost the same.

<Tip>
  Use the org dashboard to see which team members are most active and where research effort is concentrated across your portfolio coverage.
</Tip>
