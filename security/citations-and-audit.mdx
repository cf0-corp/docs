cf0's trust posture rests on one principle: **every quantitative claim has a source, and the source is one click away.** This page covers how citations are generated, how to inspect them, how to export an audit trail, and what your compliance team gets.

## How citations work

For every number Lab surfaces — revenue, margin, growth rate, multiple, free cash flow — cf0 stores the source it came from at extraction time. The citation captures:

- **Company and filing type** — e.g. `NVDA · 10-K`
- **Filing date and period covered** — `filed 2025-02-21, FY2025`
- **The specific section** — `Part II · Item 7 · MD&A`
- **A pointer to the exact passage** — page and paragraph in the filing

For narrative claims — risk-factor quotes, MD&A commentary, transcript excerpts — Lab reads the raw text and quotes it verbatim where precision matters. The quote is stored alongside the citation, not just the section header.

## Inspecting a citation

In Lab and in generated reports, citations appear as inline references next to the figure. Click the reference to open the source pane on the right:

- The relevant section opens to the cited passage, with the figure highlighted.
- The filing metadata (company, filing type, date, period) shows at the top of the pane.
- For tables, the cell coordinates highlight (e.g. row "Free cash flow", column "Q4 FY2025").

If a number doesn't have a citation — for example, a derived metric computed from cited inputs — the derivation is shown explicitly, with each input citation linked. cf0 doesn't surface uncited figures.

## Exporting an audit trail

Every Lab thread can be exported as a compliance-ready audit trail. The export captures:

- The full conversation (user prompts and agent responses)
- Every tool call and its result
- Every assumption gate and how it was resolved
- Every citation, with the source link
- Timestamps and the model version that ran each turn

To export from Lab:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the thread">
    Find the thread in the sidebar and open it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export the audit trail">
    Click the thread menu and choose **Export audit trail**. Pick Markdown or PDF.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach to your committee or compliance review">
    The audit trail accompanies the polished report. The report is what the committee reads; the trail is what survives review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Programmatically: `GET /api/lab/threads/{thread_id}/audit-trail?format=pdf` — see the [Lab API](/api/lab#audit-trail).

## What regulators and compliance teams get

- **Provenance for every figure**: traceable to the exact filing page.
- **Source-of-truth reads**: cf0 reads SEC filings and structured data feeds; the agent doesn't invent numbers, and uncited figures are not surfaced.
- **Methodology transparency**: the assumption gates (WACC, growth, terminal multiple) are captured in the audit trail along with the user's accept/override choice.
- **Model attribution**: every turn records the underlying model used (currently `us.anthropic.claude-sonnet-4-6` and `us.anthropic.claude-haiku-4-5`). Model versions are tracked.

## Data isolation

- **Org-scoped**: research threads, uploaded documents, generated reports, and saved skills live within your organisation's workspace. There is no cross-org visibility.
- **Thread privacy within an org**: thread bodies are user-scoped. Org admins see aggregate activity (counts per member) on the [Dashboard](/workspace/dashboard), but never the contents of an analyst's threads.
- **Authentication**: every session is scoped to the active organisation via Clerk. Tokens are short-lived (~1 minute) and refreshed automatically.

## Retention

- Threads, documents, and reports persist while your subscription is active.
- Thread deletion is immediate from the user's view; underlying storage is purged on the next sync cycle (within 24 hours).
- Account deletion removes the user's threads, profile, and personal documents. Org-shared documents are not affected by individual account deletion.

## What cf0 will not do

These constraints exist by design so the platform stays trustworthy under regulatory review:

- **No financial advice.** cf0 produces research and analysis; it doesn't tell you to buy or sell.
- **No model-generated math.** Calculations route through deterministic backend templates (DCF, LBO, comps, sensitivity matrices). The LLM frames the question; the math comes from a tested code path.
- **No training on your data.** cf0 never uses your firm's research, documents, or threads to train any model.
- **No uncited claims.** If the agent can't ground a figure in a source, it surfaces the gap rather than hallucinating.
